# Professional Tone (Basic)

Rewrite casual or rough text into clear, formal, professional language suitable for business and official contexts. This is the **minimalist** version: the core register shifts, without turning the prose stiff.

## When to use
- "Make this more professional / formal / official" / "this is too casual"
- Business emails, reports, proposals, official letters, announcements, executive updates

**Not for:** warm/empathetic messaging (use the empathetic-tone skill), legal documents (use legal-writing), or contexts where casual is the right call.

## The principles
1. **Formal register:** reduce slang and casual idioms; spell out where appropriate ("cannot" vs "can't" in the most formal pieces, though contractions are fine for ordinary business email).
2. **Precise and concise.** Replace vague or padded phrasing with specific, direct statements.
3. **Courteous and measured.** Polite, neutral, no sarcasm or emoji; confident without being arrogant.
4. **Structured.** Lead with purpose; use clear paragraphs or short headed sections; one idea per paragraph.
5. **Active and clear** by default; passive only when the actor is irrelevant.
6. **Consistent formatting:** proper salutations and sign-offs, correct titles, no ALL-CAPS shouting.

## Techniques
- Swap casual openers ("Hey, just wanted to…") for purposeful ones ("I'm writing to…").
- Replace fillers ("kind of", "a lot", "stuff", "ASAP") with precise terms ("somewhat", "considerable", "items", "by [date]").
- Turn vents into neutral statements of fact and request.
- Close with a clear next step and a professional sign-off.

## Before / after
**Before:** Hey — so the thing's kinda late and it's a big problem lol. Can you fix it ASAP??
**After:** Hello [Name], I wanted to flag that the deliverable is now behind schedule, which is affecting the wider timeline. Could you share a revised completion date by end of day? Thank you for your help.

**Before:** We crushed it this quarter, numbers are awesome.
**After:** The team delivered strong results this quarter, with performance exceeding our targets across key metrics.

## Don't
Formal isn't pompous. Avoid jargon pile-ups ("synergize cross-functional deliverables"), needless length, or all-passive constructions. Aim for polished and clear, not bureaucratic.
