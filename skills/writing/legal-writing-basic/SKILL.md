# Legal Writing (Basic)

Draft and polish legal/policy text in precise, formal legal English. This is the **minimalist** version: the core conventions plus skeletons for the two most-requested documents.

## ⚠️ Not legal advice
This produces a **drafting aid**, not legal advice. Laws differ by jurisdiction and change over time. A qualified lawyer must review before use, especially for privacy (GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, etc.) and consumer-protection rules. Always leave clearly marked placeholders (`[ENTITY]`, `[JURISDICTION]`, `[EFFECTIVE DATE]`) rather than inventing facts.

## When to use
- "Write a privacy policy / terms and conditions / disclaimer / NDA"
- Turning plain descriptions into formal contractual or policy language

**Not for:** giving legal conclusions, asserting what's "compliant," or filling in real legal facts the user hasn't provided.

## Legal drafting conventions
1. **Define terms once, capitalize them after:** "Service", "User", "Company" — define in a Definitions section, then reuse exactly.
2. **Use operative verbs precisely:** *shall*/*must* (obligation), *may* (permission), *will* (future), *must not* (prohibition). Be consistent.
3. **Remove ambiguity.** Avoid "etc.", "and/or" where it matters; prefer "including without limitation".
4. **Identify the parties** and the agreement up front; use recitals ("WHEREAS") only where they add context.
5. **Number clauses and sub-clauses** (1, 1.1, 1.1.1) for cross-reference.
6. **Standard boilerplate:** governing law & jurisdiction, severability, entire agreement, amendments, limitation of liability, termination, notices.
7. **Plain-language summaries** are welcome alongside (not instead of) the operative text where consumer-facing.

## Document skeletons
**Privacy Policy:** Introduction & scope · Data we collect · How/why we use it (purposes & legal bases) · Sharing & third parties · Cookies & tracking · Data retention · Your rights · International transfers · Security · Children · Changes · Contact.

**Terms & Conditions:** Acceptance · Definitions · Eligibility · Accounts · License/use rights · Acceptable use & prohibitions · Payments/subscriptions (if any) · Intellectual property · Disclaimers & warranties · Limitation of liability · Indemnity · Termination · Governing law & disputes · Changes · Contact.

## Before / after
**Before:** You can't sue us if something goes wrong with the app.
**After:** To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, the Company shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential damages arising out of or in connection with the use of, or inability to use, the Service.

## Always include
A header with `[ENTITY]`, `[EFFECTIVE DATE]`, `[JURISDICTION]` placeholders and a closing note: *"Draft for review by qualified legal counsel — not legal advice."*
