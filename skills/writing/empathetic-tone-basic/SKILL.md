# Empathetic Tone (Basic)

Rewrite text so it lands with warmth and care — acknowledging the reader's feelings and supporting them, without losing honesty. This is the **minimalist** version: the core moves, applied with a light hand.

## When to use
- "Make this warmer / softer / kinder" / "add empathy" / "this sounds cold"
- Support replies, apologies, bad news, reminders, health/coaching, sensitive topics

**Not for:** sugar-coating that hides the truth, manipulative reassurance, or contexts that need blunt precision (safety warnings, legal, incident facts).

## The principles
1. **Acknowledge first.** Name the feeling or situation before the fix: "That sounds frustrating — let's sort it out."
2. **Lead with the person, not the policy.** "I want to help you" before "Our policy states".
3. **Validate without judging.** "That makes sense" / "You're right to ask."
4. **Soften directives.** "You'll need to…" → "When you get a moment, you can…".
5. **Use warm, plain words** and "you/we". Short reassurances beat flowery ones.
6. **Take shared ownership.** "We got this wrong" instead of "the error occurred."
7. **Close with support.** Offer a next step and openness: "I'm here if anything's unclear."

## Techniques
- **Acknowledge → reassure → help → invite.** A reliable shape for hard messages.
- Replace blame ("you forgot to…") with neutral framing ("it looks like the step was missed").
- Turn "no" into a path: "I can't do X, but here's what I can do."
- Keep sentences calm and unhurried; avoid exclamation overload.

## Before / after
**Before:** Your request is denied. You did not follow the required steps.
**After:** Thanks for sending this over. It looks like a couple of steps were missed, so I can't approve it just yet — but I'll walk you through exactly what's needed so we can get it sorted.

**Before:** The product is out of stock. Check back later.
**After:** I'm sorry — this one's sold out right now, and I know that's disappointing. I can let you know the moment it's back, or suggest a close alternative if that helps.

## Don't overdo it
Warmth isn't gushing. Avoid toxic positivity ("everything happens for a reason!"), fake intimacy, or burying the actual answer under softeners. Be kind **and** clear.
